Eiffel tower

The Eiffel tower (French: La Tour Eiffel) is an iron lattice tower locaded in Champ de Mars, Paris. It was constructed in 1889 and since then has become an icon of France since, as well as one of the tallest known buildings in the world. The tower is 324 meters high, that is equivalent to approximately 81 storey building. When the tower was opened, it became the tallest building in the world, surpassing the Washington monument.

History

The tower was built between 1887 and 1889. It was designed by Gustave Eiffel, in honour to the Wold Expo 1889, which took place in Paris. Three hundred construction workers were employed in site. Due to very strict safety precautions, only one man died during the construction.

The tower consists of 18038 tempered iron pieces that are braced together using about two and a half million rivets.

Since the beginning of 20th century the tower has been used for TV and radio transmission. Two restaurants are located at the tower: "Altitude 95" (95 meters above sea level) and "Jules Verne", an expensive restaurant with a private elevator.

Event chronology

  • 1930 loses the title of world's tallest building, when the Chrysler building is finished in New York, USA.
  • 1925 to 1934 the tower becomes the largest advertisment board in the world as it illuminated signs of Citroën adorn it from three of the four sides.
  • January 3, 1956 fire damages top floors of the tower
  • 1959 the first antenna is placed on the tower
  • 2000 four high-power searchlights are installed on the tower, light shows take place from the tower since then

Taking pictures of the tower

Even though the tower itself is a public building, its illumination may be considered "original work of visual arts" and therefore can be subject to copyright. In January 2005 the director of SNTE (Société nouvelle d'exploitation de la tour Eiffel) Stéphane Dieu commented the situation: "It is really just a way to manage commercial use of the image, so that it isn't used in ways we don't approve.". But in fact the restriction prohibits publication of pictures taken by the tourists at night.

When you look for such pictures in Google, you can easily find that many people have ignored the restrictions of SNTE, therefore pictures of good quality can be found.

What's in the press

CBS News: Bomb threat briefly closes Eiffel Tower

In a March 23, 2011 article, CBS News writes about evacuation of Eiffel tower due to bombing threats.

Paris police say: bomb alert at the Eiffel Tower has been lifted and the monument is being reopened to visitors. Police and tower officials said a suspicious package and phoned-in bomb threat prompted the Eiffel Tower to evacuate nearly 4,000 tourists from the Paris landmark on Wednesday. A police official said a thorough check of the site found no explosives, and the alert was lifted.

Tourists were allowed back on the sandy plaza beneath the tower after a little more than two hours, and the ticket office is being reopened. A Paris police official said nearly 4,000 tourists were evacuated from the site after an anonymous caller warned that the Paris monument would be blown up at 1700 GMT. Other officials at the tower site then spotted a suspicious package on the ground-level plaza. Both officials spoke on condition of anonymity, saying the situation is still being investigated.

The site is occasionally evacuated because of threats. In the most recent major incident, a similar bomb threat caused the tower to be evacuated last September.
